Saving Grace Industries

Our organization’s goal is to help formerly incarcerated individuals re-enter society after prison and remove barriers that limit them from building their lives.

Saving Grace Industries In The News

Empowering Stories

Sister Mary Sean Hodges, a former Catholic school teacher and founder of Saving Grace Industries, a Restorative Justice Program which provides in-prison coaching and self-development courses, prepares for a 20th anniversary party for her organization at St. Basil Catholic Church in Los Angeles on Friday, August 12, 2022.

Program Successes

MALVERN -- Two years ago Gov. Asa Hutchinson stood in a boardroom outside his office, pointed to a display board and hailed re-entry centers as a key to the state's overpopulated prisons. This month, inmates at the state's largest re-entry center stood in front of state officials and their peers and gave testimony to the healing powers the center's programming has had on their lives. "I ain't never thought I'd have a job for this long of time." "This is what I needed to become a grown man." "This is the first time anyone has ever given me a chance."

Moving Forward

Prisoner reentry was not “discovered” until the last ten years, when it started to dominate American policy. This is caused in part by the enormous number of prisoners who have been released, as well as a movement that has identified the problem as “reentry.”
